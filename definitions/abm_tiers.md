# ABM Tier Definitions — Revenue Runway

ABM Tiers define the strategic priority of an account (Company).
They determine how much personalization and human time an account receives.

Tiers are assigned at the account level and are independent of pipeline stage or lifecycle.

---

## Tier 1 — Strategic Accounts (1:1 ABM)

### Definition
Highest priority accounts with the strongest fit and highest expected value.

### Qualification Criteria (use 2+)
- High revenue potential (ACV/LTV) relative to your offer
- Strong ICP alignment (industry + problem + urgency)
- Clear access to decision maker and buying committee
- Strategic value (lighthouse logo, case study, partner leverage)

### Engagement Model
- Highly personalized outreach
- Executive involvement
- Custom solutioning and account plans
- Fast follow-up SLAs

### Typical Volume
Low (a “named account list”).

---

## Tier 2 — High-Fit Scale Accounts (1:few ABM)

### Definition
Strong-fit accounts with meaningful value but less need for bespoke effort.

### Qualification Criteria (use 2+)
- Solid ICP fit and verified need
- Medium/high value opportunity
- Clear path to the right stakeholders
- Timing may be near-term or mid-term

### Engagement Model
- Semi-personalized sequences and assets
- Targeted outreach by role (Decision Maker/Champion)
- Periodic human touches (not fully bespoke)

### Typical Volume
Moderate.

---

## Tier 3 — Programmatic Accounts (1:many)

### Definition
Broad ICP-adjacent or early-stage accounts where you run automated nurture and lighter outbound.

### Qualification Criteria (use 1+)
- Possible fit but uncertain urgency
- Lower expected value
- Limited intel or low engagement so far

### Engagement Model
- Automated nurture
- Light personalization
- Trigger-based follow-up when behavior indicates intent

### Typical Volume
High.

---

## Not Rated

### Definition
Default state when an account has not yet been tiered.

### Guidance
Accounts should move from Not Rated to a tier once basic qualification is known.
